Finding

No heat at all on the Lister card. Inside Renter, the hottest single point sits on the “Wedding” example tag — before the mode itself is confirmed.

Hottest point on the screen is the “Wedding Guest” quick-add chip, not the free-text brief field. Chat bubble and “Make my look” get moderate, secondary heat.

Dense hotspot on the top-left tile only. Bottom row gets zero heat. Light heat on the filter pills.

Heavy heat on the “Front” thumbnail and “Select” CTA. Side / Detail / Fabric tabs get comparatively little. Zero heat on Chest/Waist fields — expected, static text isn’t a tap target either way.

Two hotspots on the rental-dates block — engagement with pickup/return as part of confirming, not a stuck point (dates worked; where response was incomplete, that was prototype interactivity, not a design issue). Zero heat near the price line, deposit, or delivery text.

Recommendation

Those tags read as decorative previews, not live filters, at this stage. Likely a false affordance — either wire them to jump straight to occasion entry, or visually de-emphasize until mode is confirmed.

Users default to tapping a tag over typing a brief — matches transcript behavior (553293903 narrates tags, not prose). Treat quick-add as the primary path, free text as optional.

Choice happens fast, from position 1 — nobody engaged row 2 before acting. Ranking of the first match carries outsized weight; a weak top result may mean no second chance before drop-off.

People check the front photo, mostly skip other angles, then commit. Doesn’t confirm or deny fit-proof sufficiency (H3) alone — but the review is partial, not exhaustive, and that’s worth knowing.

The interface gives no tap target on the total — no breakdown, nothing to expand — on the exact screen where “what the hell is service fee” happens in the transcripts. Direct support for making the total inspectable, not just earlier (H5).

Note: these are click/interaction heatmaps (Maze), not gaze or attention data — heat reflects where people tapped, not where they looked. No heat on static text (price lines, fit-proof labels) reflects the absence of a tap target there, not whether it was read. Findings are aggregated across sessions; no per-participant click log was available, so observations are worded to the confidence the heatmap supports rather than expressed as participant counts.
